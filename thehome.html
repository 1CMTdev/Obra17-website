Visual & Performing Arts Production Unit

OBRA17: Tenacity of Society is an art gallery that seeks to utilize art as an instrument to draw attention to the different social issues in the Philippines. Altogether, the organization advocates social consciousness in the midst of isolation—with the intension is inspiring change.

ARTWORK OF THE DAY

Ako Bahala by Paige Jastty Sales

Ako Bahala, a phrase that can either kill or save someone. This artwork represents the relevance of inequality within social classes as people fight their way into survival. In a world where disparities emerge, some may be blind, some may be deaf, but one thing is for sure—only few are alive.

Inspirational Message from the Heads of Obra

In line with the purpose to draw attention to the different social issues in the Philippines, OBRA 17: Tenacity of Society serves as an eye-opener and mechanism to advocate social consciousness to everyone. Through art, one will be able to understand the power of recalibrating a certain issue by focusing on what truly matters. It is easy to get lost in our own predispositions as we are nestled in the comfort of our homes. The faces of the priveleged are painted with comfort, while there are those who struggle at this moment. What happened to the oppressed, homeless, and indigent? Their voices remain unheard due to prevailing forms of injustice.

OBRA 17: Tenacity of Society is a call for artists to illustrate the concerns of our community, giving emphasis to the needs of the people. We believe that the gallery will inspire its audience to gain awareness and compassion to the subjects, as well promote the art of exploration. Let us be put in the situation where we can unleash our creative juices while being able to influence other people.
